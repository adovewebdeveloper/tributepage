Viktor Emil Frankl (26 March 1905 â€“ 2 September 1997)
was an Austrian neurologist and psychiatrist.
A Holocaust survivor, he was the founder of logotherapy (literally "healing through meaning") - a meaning-centered school of psychotherapy, considered the Third Viennese School of Psychotherapy - following the theories developed by Sigmund Freud and Alfred Adler.
Logotherapy is part of existential and humanistic psychology theories. He is the author of over 39 books; he is most noted for his best-selling book Man's Search for Meaning based on his experiences in various Nazi concentration camps.
